03 — Intelligence levels

Instant, Medium, and High

Use the speed tier to match the risk and complexity of the job. Faster is not always better when reasoning depth matters.

Level Best for Why it fits
Instant Quick drafting, summaries, simple rewrites, translation, and light brainstorming. You want a fast answer and the cost of a shallow pass is low.
Medium Planning, debugging, document review, comparisons, and complex explanations. It balances speed and deeper thinking for most serious everyday work.
High Architecture, multi-step analysis, security reviews, proofs, and high-stakes decisions. It gives the model more room to reason carefully before answering.

Ready-to-copy prompt starters

Quick answer

Deep audit

Use this when accuracy, completeness, and contradiction-hunting matter more than speed.

Audit this carefully. Find contradictions, missing dependencies, weak assumptions, and edge cases. Explain your reasoning plainly.

Clarification first

Use this when the task is ambiguous or when you do not want the model to assume anything.

Everyday default

Use a balanced reasoning setting for most serious work, especially writing, planning, and mixed tasks.

Fast mode

Deep audit mode
